I am a theoretical and computational chemist specializing in quantum‑chemistry approaches to tackle challenging problems in chemical physics. My work aims to bridge method development and experimentally observable phenomena.

I completed my doctoral research at IISER Kolkata, focusing on Density Functional Theory (DFT) and Time‑Dependent DFT (TDDFT). I was the lead developer of InDFT, an in‑house code for modeling atoms and molecules with low‑scaling techniques.

As a postdoctoral researcher at TIFR Mumbai, I explored non‑Hermitian quantum mechanics to study negative ion resonances and complex absorbing potentials. Currently, I am a research fellow at Northwestern University, investigating auto‑ionizing excited states and their spectroscopic signatures.

My research interests include:

  • Density Functional Theory and Time‑Dependent DFT
  • Non‑Hermitian quantum mechanics
  • Auto‑ionizing and metastable excited states
  • Spectroscopy and excited‑state dynamics

About Me

As As a physical chemist, my research is rooted in theoretical and quantum chemistry, with a strong focus on developing new theoretical frameworks, computational methodologies, and efficient algorithms to address pressing challenges in chemistry, material science and nanoscience.

I completed my doctoral research at IISER Kolkata, where I worked extensively on Density Functional Theory (DFT) and Time-Dependent Density Functional Theory (TDDFT) which now become workhorse across diverse areas of modern chemical research.During this period, I served as the lead developer of InDFT, an in-house electronic structure package designed for efficient atomistic and molecular simulations.

Areas of Specialization

1. Electronic Structure Theory

Development and computational implementation of Density Functional Theory (DFT), Random-Phase Approximation (RPA), and various range-separated exchange-correlation functionals.

2. Non-Linear Electron Dynamics

Development and computational implementation of real-time Time-Dependent Density Functional Theory (RT-TDDFT) for studying electron dynamics in the presence of intense laser fields.

3. Excited States and Non-Radiative Processes

  • Charge-transfer states in TADF systems
  • Inverted singlet-triplet splitting and reverse intersystem crossing
  • X-ray induced core-electron excited states
  • Absorption spectra of point defects in materials

4. Non-Hermitian Quantum Mechanics

Development and computational implementation of complex Density Functional Theory for the accurate and computationally efficient characterization of metastable anions formed through low-energy electron–molecule collisions. These transient states play important roles in chemical processes spanning interstellar chemistry, radiation chemistry, and biomolecular systems.

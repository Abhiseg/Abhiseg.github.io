About Me

I am a theoretical and computational chemist with research interests at the interface of quantum chemistry, chemical physics, and electronic-structure theory. My work focuses on developing and applying advanced quantum-mechanical frameworks to understand electronically excited states, resonances, and non-Hermitian phenomena in molecular systems.

I completed my doctoral research at IISER Kolkata, where I was deeply involved in the development of Density Functional Theory (DFT) and Time-Dependent DFT (TDDFT) methodologies. I was the lead developer of InDFT, an in-house electronic-structure code for atoms and molecules, contributing low-scaling approaches for estimating fundamental gaps and modeling charge-transfer excitations.

Following my PhD, I worked as a postdoctoral researcher at TIFR Mumbai, where my research centered on the theoretical characterization of negative ion resonances using non-Hermitian Hamiltonians, complex absorbing potentials, and orbital-dependent exchange–correlation functionals.

I am currently a research fellow at Northwestern University, where my work explores auto-ionizing excited states and their accurate description within modern quantum-chemical frameworks. More broadly, I am interested in bridging methodological developments with experimentally observable phenomena, particularly in spectroscopy and ultrafast dynamics.

Research Interests

  • Density Functional Theory and Time-Dependent DFT
  • Non-Hermitian quantum mechanics and resonances
  • Auto-ionizing and metastable excited states
  • Electronic-structure method development
  • Spectroscopy and excited-state dynamics

About Me

I am a theoretical and computational chemist specializing in quantum chemistry and electronic structure theory, with a strong interest in developing theoretical frameworks, computational methodologies, and efficient algorithms for chemically relevant quantum many-body problems.

I completed my doctoral research at IISER Kolkata, where I worked extensively on Density Functional Theory (DFT) and Time-Dependent Density Functional Theory (TDDFT). During this period, I served as the lead developer of InDFT, an in-house electronic structure package designed for efficient atomistic and molecular simulations.

As a postdoctoral researcher at TIFR Mumbai, I explored non-Hermitian quantum mechanics, complex absorbing potential approaches, and negative ion resonances.

Currently, I am a research fellow at Northwestern University, where my research focuses on autoionizing excited states, advanced spectroscopy simulations, and scalable many-body electronic structure methods.

Research Interests

  • Density Functional Theory (DFT)
  • Time-Dependent DFT (TDDFT)
  • Many-Body Green's Function Methods
  • Metastable and Autoionizing States
  • Non-Hermitian Quantum Mechanics
  • Excited-State Spectroscopy
